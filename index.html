Cyber Plant Camp

Using technology to keep plants alive!

Technology employed:

Python coding, Raspberry Pi 3's, and Arduino Unos

Knowledge and skills development:

Plant physiology, python coding, and computer engineering.

Audience:

Students grades 5-7

Course overview:

Over the course of three days, middle school students will build a Raspberry Pi moisture sensor to monitor a plant. They will apply basic concepts related to plant physiology and programming to develop a choice of three systems. All three combine the use of Python, Arduino Unos, Rasperry Pi 3's, and a potted plant. The three systems either use an Arduino to sense soil moisture, sense light levels, or both. The Raspberry Pi displays the moisture readings on its screen, and may send messages to the students when plant watering is needed, if time permits.
